About Wonder

Wonder is an immersive, story-driven educational game designed to enhance learning for youth and support English comprehension for non-native speakers. Set in a vibrant digital art world, players engage in interactive adventures, STEM activities, and literacy challenges. The game integrates AI and a culturally inclusive approach, aligning with educational curricula to create an engaging and effective learning experience.

Multilingual Literacy

Wonder features an AI-powered Reading Assistant that supports multiple languages, catering to students with varying English proficiency levels. Real-time translation and text-to-speech capabilities enable players to effortlessly navigate content and enhance comprehension. An advanced captioning system highlights key concepts with interactive, color-coded keywords that display definitions when clicked. Additionally, a comprehensive dictionary allows instant lookup of new words and phrases, making language learning intuitive and enjoyable for all students. These features expand English vocabulary and understanding, benefiting both native and non-native speakers through an immersive, inclusive learning experience.

Hands-On STEM Learning

Wonder introduces students to STEM concepts through hands-on, story-driven challenges. Using intuitive block-based coding, kids engage in virtual building and problem-solving tasks that help the story protagonist overcome obstacles. These interactive challenges make STEM learning accessible, supporting the development of problem-solving skills and basic STEM knowledge in a fun, narrative-driven way that appeals to diverse learners.

Educational Integration

Wonder seeks to integrate education curriculum in collaboration with educational experts, for instance, to align with provincial standards and enable educators to integrate it into classrooms and extracurricular programs. To assess impact on student engagement and literacy outcomes, the game will incorporate research and feedback loops to highlight progress. The focus of Wonder is to benefit student learning as a versatile tool for educational settings.
